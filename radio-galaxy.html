Radio.Galaxy

Interaction Design, 3rd semester, 2022/23

In 2017 radio telescopes all over the world observed the center of a galaxy far far away…and finally got the first ever image of a black hole. Many know of this spectacular picture, but only very few know what it took to actually get it. Radio.Galaxy is a project, currently still in development, aimed at explaining the complicated image creation process to the general public. In collaboration with the chair of astronomy at the University of Würzburg a prototype called “Black Hole Cam” was developed, which provides information on the technology behind the black hole image and lets the user simulate their own picture of a black hole.

Based on the initial design the project got awarded a 10.000€ prize money from the federal ministry of science and education as part of the Science Year 2023 initiative. The prize money is now being used to develop a full-scale AR-game.

Currently in development: Radio.Galaxy

Radio.Galaxy will lean even more into gamification and will feature a black hole character, that guides the user through the process of creating an image. Instead of a digital map, Radio.Galaxy will have a physical map on which the user will be able to place 3D-modelled radio telescopes that can be analyzed with an AR-feature. This project will be finished by the end of 2023.
